A few facts about Trinidad and Tobago

Trinidad is the larger and more populous of the two major islands of Trinidad and Tobago. The island lies 11 km (6.8 mi) off the northeastern coast of Venezuela and sits on the continental shelf of South America.

Main facts about Trinidad and Tobago

  1. A man named Christopher Columbus named the country "Trinidad" in 1498 during his third voyage in the New World

  2. Trinidad and Tobago Carnival is one of the top ten carnival celebrations in the world

  3. People from Tobago are called Tobagonians while those from Trinidad are called Trinidadians or Trinis.
